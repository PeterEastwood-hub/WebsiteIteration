Life at Nearform

Nearformers don’t have to choose between life and work. Remote-first since the start, Nearform has a model that lets people work where they live, rewarding both our teams and our clients with a breadth of perspectives. We may be far apart, but deep collaboration connects us.

Flexible hours

We empower teams to decide their core working hours, so they can be together when it suits them. Work fits around their lives, not the other way round.

Work wherever

We give our team the chance to temporarily relocate anywhere for up to 90 days. Remote first means we can maximise flexibility!

Always progressing

We provide generous access to training, letting our people drive their own progress, whether through knowledge sharing or Open Source projects.

Apart but connected

No consultant is an island. Our teams stay connected through highly collaborative teamwork on every client project.

Meet Ups

We bring our people together whenever we can. From our legendary Nearfest gatherings to team co-working days and local community dinners, we value connection and social time. We may be remote first, but we definitely are not remote only!

FAQ

We work remotely with our main HQ based in Tramore, the south of Ireland. Each job description will say where the role is usually based, though we’re big fans of flexible working!

We don’t accept email applications. If there’s a role which interests you please submit an application via the relevant job description above. If there aren’t any roles which are the right fit at the moment, please follow our social profiles to keep up to date with new roles.

If a role isn’t on our website it means we’re not accepting applications for the position anymore. We process applications on a rolling basis and encourage everyone to apply as soon as they can. We’re growing super fast though, so sign up for our email updates to be the first to hear about new roles at Nearform.

We’re not looking for any recruitment support right now. We’ve got a big enough team to hire most of the roles we need. But we’ll let you know if that changes!
